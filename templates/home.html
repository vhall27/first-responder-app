Resources & Support
You don’t have to navigate this alone
Consider saving a few of these options before or after you take a screening.
Support available 24/7
Crisis Support (U.S.)
Phone / Text / Chat
Call or text 988 in the U.S. for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, or visit 988lifeline.org.
Peer Support / EAP
Local / Department resources
Many departments have a peer support team or Employee Assistance Program (EAP). If it’s safe to do so, consider reaching out to a trusted supervisor, union rep, or peer-support lead.
First Responder–Focused Therapists
Therapy / Counseling
Look for clinicians who specifically mention experience with first responders, PTSD, or trauma-focused therapies. If available, your EAP or union may keep a vetted list.
If you feel unsafe right now
If you’re thinking about self-harm, feel like you might hurt someone, or are otherwise in immediate danger:
Call 988 (U.S.) Contact local emergency services Reach out to someone you trust
This tool is for education and self-reflection only and does not provide a diagnosis or replace talking with a licensed mental health professional. If you are thinking about self-harm, harming others, or feel unsafe, contact local emergency services or a crisis line immediately.
